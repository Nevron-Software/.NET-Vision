This example demonstrates the primitive 1D Shapes, which are: NLineShape, NCircularArcShape, NEllipticalArcShape, NPolylineShape, NBezierCurveShape and NClosedCurveShape.

Common for primitive 1D shapes is that they all aggregate a single open figure path primitive of the respective type. For example the NLineShape aggregates a single NLinePath primitive.

Open figure paths can be configured to display arrowheads at their start and end points. Arrowheads can have their own fill and stroke styles and automatically follow the orientation of the path at its start/end position respectively. Arrowheads adopt the shadow style of the path for which their are displayed. The arrowheads of a path are displayed with the composed StartArrowheadStyle and EndArrowheadStyle respecively.

Open figure paths can be displayed with no arrowheads at all - in this example all random strokes are displayed without arrowheads.

By design primitive 1D Shapes have a single logical line label anchored to the shape itself. To change the label text just click on the shape and type.
