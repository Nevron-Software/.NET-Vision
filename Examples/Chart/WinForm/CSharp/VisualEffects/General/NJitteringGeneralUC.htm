Jittering is a special visual effect that enables you to antialias the whole scene. The idea behind it is very simple - the control renders several samples of the image each having a small offset in the X and Y direction. These samples are then added together to produce the final image. Note that jittering slows down the rendering speed.
Check the Enable jittering check box to turn on the jittering.
The Jittering steps combo controls the number of samples.
From the Jittering deviation up down you can control the maximum offset along the X and Y direction of the generated samples from the original image.
